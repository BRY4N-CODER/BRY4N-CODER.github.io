1940: Classic Comic Joker

First appeared in Batman #1, created by Bill Finger, Bob Kane, and Jerry Robinson.

1966: Cesar Romero's Joker

Played in the Batman TV series; a more lighthearted, campy version of the Joker.

1989: Jack Nicholson's Joker

Portrayed in Tim Burton's Batman; a darker, villainous version of the character.

1992: Mark Hamill's Animated Joker

Started voicing the Joker in Batman: The Animated Series, a menacing yet charismatic take.

2008: Heath Ledger's Joker

Portrayed in The Dark Knight; an anarchic and unpredictable force of chaos.

2009: Arkham Game Joker

Featured as the main antagonist in the Arkham series of video games, a more devious and intelligent version.

2016: Jared Leto's Joker

A gangster-inspired, manic portrayal in Suicide Squad, with tattoos and a flashy look.

2015: Cameron Monaghan's Gotham Joker

Played Jerome and Jeremiah Valeska in Gotham, whose characters were inspired by the Joker's chaotic nature.

2019: Joaquin Phoenix's Joker

A deep dive into the tragic origin of Arthur Fleck in the movie Joker, exploring mental illness and societal neglect.

Various: Animated Jokers

Appears in various animated series, each with different takes on the character's personality.

2022: The Batman's Joker

Introduced in The Batman movie, with an unknown actor but a disturbing, grotesque portrayal.

2013: Injustice Joker

A brutal, twisted version of the Joker from the Injustice video game series, who plays a key role in the multiverse conflict.

2011: New 52 Joker

A more sadistic and grotesque version, with his face cut off in the comics and worn as a mask.
